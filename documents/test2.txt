Artificial intelligence is transforming drug discovery and development across the pharmaceutical industry. Machine learning models are being used to predict molecular behavior, identify potential drug candidates, and optimize clinical trial designs. The FDA has issued guidance on the use of AI and machine learning in medical device software, signaling growing regulatory acceptance of these technologies. However, challenges remain around data quality, model interpretability, and validation requirements. Pharmaceutical companies must demonstrate that AI systems used in regulated processes are fit for purpose and produce reliable results. The concept of algorithmic accountability is emerging as a key consideration, requiring organizations to document how AI models make decisions and ensure those decisions can be audited. As AI adoption accelerates, regulatory frameworks are evolving to keep pace with technological advancement.
