The FDA's 21 CFR Part 11 regulation establishes the criteria under which electronic records and electronic signatures are considered trustworthy, reliable, and equivalent to paper records. The regulation applies to any FDA-regulated industry that creates, modifies, maintains, archives, retrieves, or transmits electronic records. Key requirements include the implementation of audit trails that capture who did what and when, access controls that limit system access to authorized individuals, and validation of computer systems to ensure they perform as intended. Organizations must also maintain procedures for use and oversight of electronic signature systems. Failure to comply with Part 11 can result in FDA warning letters, import alerts, and consent decrees. The regulation has become increasingly important as pharmaceutical and medical device companies transition from paper-based to electronic systems for managing critical quality and regulatory data.
